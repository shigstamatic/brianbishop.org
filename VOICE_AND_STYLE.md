# Voice And Style

This site should feel like a clear public record of work, not a marketing site.

## Overall voice

Preferred qualities:

- Plainspoken
- Observant
- Specific
- Calm
- Lightly technical when useful
- Comfortable with unfinished work
- Honest about constraints, tradeoffs, and uncertainty

Avoid:

- Marketing language
- Inflated claims
- Forced profundity
- Startup-style enthusiasm
- Overexplaining obvious things
- Turning every post into an essay

## Point of view

Use first person when it sounds natural.

Good:

```text
I wanted a place to keep these decisions visible.
```

Also good:

```text
This post collects the first public notes from the house project.
```

Avoid a detached corporate voice.

## Sentence style

Prefer:

- Short paragraphs
- Concrete nouns
- Active verbs
- Specific dates, materials, tools, places, constraints, and decisions
- Clear transitions

Use technical detail when it helps the reader understand the work. Do not add technical detail just to sound technical.

## Post structure

Most posts should use a simple structure:

```markdown
Opening context

What changed / what happened

Why it matters

Details, images, notes, or next steps
```

Not every post needs headings. Use headings when they make scanning easier.

## Titles

Titles should be descriptive and direct.

Good:

```text
Katy Manor: First Public Log
Foundation Layout Notes
Small Utilities And Prototypes
Why This Site Exists
```

Avoid:

```text
An Incredible Journey Begins
Unlocking The Future Of Homebuilding
Thoughts
Update
```

## Summaries

Summaries should be one or two sentences. They should explain what the post contains, not sell the post.

Good:

```text
The first public note for the Katy Manor house build, with context on what the project is and how updates will be organized.
```

## Captions

Captions should identify what the image shows and why it matters.

Good:

```text
Foundation layout marks before the next round of concrete work.
```

Avoid:

```text
Beautiful progress on an amazing day.
```

## Editing rules for Codex

When drafting from bullet points:

- Preserve the user's meaning.
- Keep the tone understated.
- Ask before inventing facts, dates, locations, measurements, prices, names, or technical details.
- Keep exact text exact when marked.
- Use public-safe wording for locations and personal details.
- Prefer a useful short post over a padded long one.

When revising exact prose:

- Improve clarity and flow.
- Do not erase the user's voice.
- Do not make the post sound more certain, grand, or polished than intended.
